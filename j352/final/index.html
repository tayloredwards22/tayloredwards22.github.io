Powerful Fandoms and their affect on Streaming: 1D & BTS

In the new age of social media, fandoms have evolved what streaming is to music. Before the digital age, people didn’t stream music for the chart numbers. If a song were doing good genuinely, it would come out on top of the charts. With the rise of fandoms and fangirls, there is new pressure on artists to have the most streams. Whether replaying a YouTube music video or having a strategic way to build streams on Spotify, fandoms have found a new way to get their artists recognized. Two popular bands who have redefined the way of streaming are One Direction and BTS. Directioners and ARMY are two of the largest fandoms in the music industry and they show love to their favorite artists in many ways. One of the ways is by streaming their songs to that they will break a record, which is a big accomplishment for the artist. Both fandoms have changed what streaming looks like. This website explores the differences between fandoms in streaming and how they do it

One Direction Stats

Let’s start with One Direction first. They were formed on X-Factor in 2010 with members, Liam, Harry, Zayn, Louis, and Niall. In their peak days when they were still together, they accomplished great streaming numbers.

According to Billboard " in 2015, they sold over 6.49 million U.S. albums. They also had nineteen total songs chart on the Hot 100.

Even after the band broke up, they’re still listened to. According to Spotify, 65 million Spotify playlists feature at least one 1D song and listeners have created more than 4.4 million playlists dedicated specifically to the band. As of July 2020, they had over 19 million listeners a month.

    BTS, also known as Bangtan Sonyeondan, was formed in 2015 in South Korea by the company Big Hit Entertainment. The group consists of members RM, Jin, SUGA, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook.

    This K-pop group wasn’t as popular when they first started out. In comparison to One Direction in 2015, their numbers were lower.

    They didn’t see success until their Wings era when there was a jump in sales.

    Since then, they have picked up a lot of accomplishments. According to Spotify, In 2019, BTS was the first group from Asia to surpass 5 billion streams on the platform.

    Although both groups have insanely dedicated fandoms, BTS has broken records when it comes to streaming because of how ARMY streams its songs. Streaming has changed so much that Billboard has changed its streaming rules. As of January 11, 2022, only 1 digital sale will be counted per customer per week for songs and albums. "Bulk purchases" of 2 or more will not be counted.
